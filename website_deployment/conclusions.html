Conclusions

With a percentage of less than 5%, we can conclude that demographics do not contribute to overall dropout rates. However, percentage of more than 80%, it is safe to conclude that poverty does play an effect into overall dropout rates.

In conclusion, we have discovered that finding a concrete reason for drop out rates is complicated. While poverty does play a part in predicted drop out rates, factors such as teacher qualifications, school funding, and student involvement can also play a part in overall drop out rates. These factors were not considered due to the fact that our team did not have the data for them.

For future analysis, some improvements that could be made would be to perform this analysis on multiple cities in America. Perhaps a more linear trend could be discovered if a plethora of city data on schools could be fed into the machine learning algorithm. Additionally, having an updated dataset would be of great help in making an official analysis.
